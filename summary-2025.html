Key Discussion Themes

Critical appraisal and clinical application insights

1
Validity & Study Design
Robust randomization and allocation concealment in REGAIN
Outcome assessors/statisticians blinded; treating teams unblinded
~15% crossovers and 11% loss to follow-up were acceptable
Key: Well-designed pragmatic RCT, not generalizable to elective THA
2
Magnitude & Clinical Meaning
No clinically important long-term differences in survival or function
Pain/opioid differences limited to POD1‚Äì60 days only
Stats taught: Cox regression, mixed-effects models, forest plots
3
Applicability to Practice
REGAIN = frail, older, urgent hip fracture patients
Elective THA = younger, optimized, ERAS protocol patients
Critical: Do NOT extrapolate REGAIN to elective cases
4
Local Context (UC Davis)
ERAS THA pathways already use neuraxial + multimodal analgesia
Barriers: Spinal feasibility in anticoagulated patients, PACU staffing
