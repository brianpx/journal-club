Faculty Guide: Anesthesiology Resident Journal Club

A Practical Framework for Teaching Critical Appraisal

1. Purpose

Journal Club provides a structured, resident-led way to teach evidence appraisal while meeting ACGME requirements for:

  • PBLI 1: Evidence-Based and Informed Practice
  • Resident Scholarship & CCC Documentation

This model emphasizes resident-driven discussion and faculty-guided application, making the best use of a 1-hour session. Resources are guides, not requirements—please adapt them as needed. For questions, contact bpitts@health.ucdavis.edu or submit feedback here.

  • Resident Scholarship & CCC Documentation

2. Format: The One-Hour Session

Session Schedule

One-hour journal club session breakdown
Time Activity Faculty Role
0–7 min Introductions, context, BLUF Prompt presenter to frame the clinical question and summarize in one sentence
7–25 min Validity Guide residents to identify design strengths, limitations, and bias
25–40 min Results Highlight interpretation beyond p-values: effect size, NNT, harms, precision
40–55 min Application Use patient scenarios to discuss how/if findings change practice
55–60 min Wrap-Up Summarize take-home points and link explicitly to PBLI milestones

3. One-Hour Journal Club Timeline

1

0–7 min • Introductions & BLUF

Resident — Responsibilities / Objectives

  • Deliver a concise BLUF (one-sentence bottom line).
  • State the clinical question (PICO/PECO) and its relevance.
  • Identify practice decision points this evidence could influence.

0–7 min

Faculty — Responsibilities / Objectives

  • Prompt a focused BLUF and align on the clinical question.
  • Set expectations for resident-led, discussion-first format.
  • Surface intended learning outcomes (PBLI) and scope.
2

7–25 min • Validity

Resident — Responsibilities / Objectives

  • Lead the internal validity review (randomization, concealment, blinding).
  • Assess baseline balance, adherence, ITT vs per-protocol.
  • Identify crossovers, missing data, and bias/confounding risks.

7–25 min

Faculty — Responsibilities / Objectives

  • Keep pace and focus; ensure the checklist is covered.
  • Probe bias implications for interpretation.
  • Model respectful challenge and evidence-based reasoning.
3

25–40 min • Results

Resident — Responsibilities / Objectives

  • Report magnitude & precision (effect sizes, CIs, absolute risk, NNT/NNH).
  • Differentiate clinical significance from statistical significance.
  • Describe harms/adverse events and time-course patterns.

25–40 min

Faculty — Responsibilities / Objectives

  • Check interpretation accuracy; ask for absolute numbers when helpful.
  • Relate endpoints to patient-centered outcomes and trade-offs.
  • Bridge metrics to decision-making under uncertainty.
4

40–55 min • Application

Resident — Responsibilities / Objectives

  • Apply findings to short clinical/operational vignettes relevant to your practice.
  • State when/for whom results are (not) applicable; name caveats.
  • Propose a concrete practice change and anticipate barriers.

40–55 min

Faculty — Responsibilities / Objectives

  • Facilitate scenario testing; encourage counterfactuals and edge cases.
  • Surface local constraints (resources, workflow, staffing) and mitigations.
  • Anchor decisions to guidelines/policy while honoring evidence limits.
5

55–60 min • Wrap-Up

Resident — Responsibilities / Objectives

  • Summarize 2–3 take-home points and one uncertainty to watch.
  • Commit to a micro-change or audit to trial next week.
  • Identify a PBLI goal and how you'll get feedback on it.

55–60 min

Faculty — Responsibilities / Objectives

  • Reinforce take-homes and the practice experiment/resident commitment.
  • Connect to evaluation (PBLI milestones) and follow-up touchpoint.
  • Assign a brief dissemination step (team share, pocket card, post).

4. Expected Outcomes for Faculty

  • Active learning: Residents drive the critique; faculty facilitate.
  • Safe climate: Residents feel comfortable challenging published conclusions.
  • Defensible documentation: Rubrics provide longitudinal data for CCC review.

5. Key Takeaway

This 1-hour, resident-led journal club is efficient, consistent, and accreditation-aligned. Faculty guide discussion and highlight application—the structure and tools do the rest.
