Reconciling the Results: Why Do These Trials Differ?

Four key factors explain the divergent findings

1

Different Generations of Antiemetics

Apfel studied first-generation 5-HT3 antagonists. Palonosetron has different pharmacokinetics that may not follow the same additive model.

2

Power to Detect Differences

Grigio's 91 patients vs Apfel's 5,161. The smaller study cannot detect the ~26% RRR each agent provides.

3

Confounding: Dexamethasone Dosing

Grigio used 8mg dexamethasone in aprepitant group vs 4mg in placebo‚Äîconfounding interpretation.

4

Ceiling Effect Consideration

When baseline prophylaxis is robust, incremental benefit from additional agents becomes harder to demonstrate.

The Null Result

No significant difference

Despite adding aprepitant (and higher-dose dexamethasone), no statistically significant reduction in PONV at any timepoint.

‚ö° The "Aha" Moment

üíé Clinical Pearls from the Room

1

Start with the Apfel Score‚ÄîBut Don't Stop There

Consider surgery type, history of motion sickness, and patient expectations. A patient who "always gets sick" deserves aggressive prophylaxis regardless of calculated score.

2

The "Why Not Both?" Trap

If you've already given ondansetron + dexamethasone + scopolamine + TIVA, the fifth agent is fighting diminishing returns. Save expensive options for rescue or delayed PONV.

3

Droperidol Deserves a Second Look

At antiemetic doses (0.625-1.25mg), the QTc risk is minimal. Multiple residents admitted they'd never used it‚Äîit's effective, cheap, and underutilized.

4

Timing Matters More Than We Think

Palonosetron's 40-hour half-life means it's particularly useful for delayed PONV. Match the drug's pharmacokinetics to the expected PONV timeline.

5

Don't Forget the Non-Pharmacologic

Adequate hydration, minimizing opioids, avoiding gastric insufflation, and even acupressure at P6 all contribute. These "free" interventions should be standard.

‚ùì Questions That Sparked Discussion

"If I've given three antiemetics and the patient still vomits in PACU, should I reach for a fourth class or repeat one that worked before?"

Consensus: Try a different mechanism first. If they've had ondansetron, dexamethasone, and scopolamine, consider promethazine or low-dose haloperidol.

"Does TIVA really make that much difference?"

Consensus: The ~19% RRR from propofol TIVA vs volatile is real and meaningful in high-risk patients. Worth it for Apfel 3-4 patients, especially ambulatory surgery.

"When is aprepitant actually worth the cost?"

Consensus: Consider for highly emetogenic chemotherapy and patients with severe PONV history despite aggressive prophylaxis. Not routine first-line given cost.

"Should we be documenting Apfel scores in our preop assessment?"

Consensus: Yes‚Äîit takes 10 seconds and creates a rational framework. Helps with handoffs and some institutions have built it into EMR templates.
